My name is Corbin Taylor and I am an Astronomy Doctorate Candidate at the University of Maryland. I have extensive research experience, working both as an observer and a theorist over the course of a decade.


My current line of research is the study of supermassive black holes, and in particular, discovering how these objects grow over cosmic time. Using my skills as a computer programmer, I work to create more physically-motivated X-ray spectral models, helping to better determine the properties of the accretion disks and of black holes themselves.
